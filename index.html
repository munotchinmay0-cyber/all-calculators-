Understanding SIP & Compounding

What is SIP?

A Systematic Investment Plan lets you invest a fixed monthly sum into mutual funds. Small, regular investments harness rupee-cost averaging and compound growth over decades — giving ordinary investors extraordinary results.

Step-Up Advantage

An annual step-up increases your SIP in line with salary growth. Stepping up by 10% per year can double or triple your final wealth compared to a flat SIP — the best way to grow wealth as your income grows.

LTCG Tax (2024)

Budget 2024 introduced 12.5% LTCG tax on equity mutual fund gains above ₹1 Lakh per year. For long-term investors, factor this in to get a realistic post-tax wealth estimate.

Understanding SWP & Wealth Sustainability

What is SWP?

SWP is the reverse of SIP — you systematically withdraw monthly income from your accumulated retirement corpus while the remaining balance continues compounding. Used by retirees for tax-efficient passive income.

The Exhaustion Point

If your withdrawal rate exceeds the fund's return rate, the corpus shrinks every month and eventually runs out. This tool shows exactly when exhaustion occurs so you can reduce withdrawals in time.

Inflation & Lifestyle

A flat ₹50,000/month withdrawal loses half its real value in 12 years at 6% inflation. Enable inflation step-up to keep your standard of living intact throughout retirement.

Lumpsum Investment Mechanics

What is Lumpsum?

Lumpsum investing means deploying a large amount at once — e.g., a bonus, inheritance, or savings — rather than spreading it monthly. The full amount starts compounding immediately, giving maximum time in market.

Power of Time

₹1 Lakh at 12% return doubles every 6 years. Over 30 years it becomes ₹30 Lakhs. Time is the most powerful variable — even a small delay of 5 years cuts final wealth by 40%.

Nominal vs Real Returns

Your nominal return is the number your statement shows. But inflation silently erodes purchasing power each year. Real return = nominal return minus inflation. Always plan in real terms to avoid undersaving.

Understanding Debt & Your Options

The True Cost of Debt

A 20-year home loan at 9% means you pay nearly 2x the principal as total interest over the tenure. Reducing just 1–2 years off this saves lakhs. Early years are when prepayments are most powerful — interest makes up 80%+ of early EMIs.

Loan vs SIP: The Real Battle

Your loan charges you a guaranteed 9% (or more). SIP offers expected 12% but that is NOT guaranteed. The conservative choice is always pay loan first. The optimistic choice is run both simultaneously. The smart choice depends on your loan rate vs SIP rate differential.

RBI Prepayment Rules (Know Your Rights)

Since 2011, RBI prohibits banks from charging prepayment fees on floating-rate home loans to individuals. For personal and car loans, penalties of 2–5% still apply on the outstanding balance. Always check your loan agreement before prepaying.

Education Loan Advantage

Government-subsidised education loans often have 0% prepayment penalty and even interest subsidies during study. Section 80E allows deduction on interest paid — reducing your effective loan cost. Always claim this tax benefit.

Strategic Goal Engineering

Backward Induction

Instead of guessing your final wealth, set the target and work backwards. The Goal Planner computes exactly what monthly SIP you need today to reach your goal — inflation-adjusted and step-up ready.

Inflation Deflection

Planning ₹1 Crore in 20 years without inflation is wrong. At 6% inflation, you actually need ₹3.2 Crore. The planner inflates your goal so you save the right amount today.

Step-Up Matching

Start with a small SIP today and increase it annually in line with salary hikes. This lets you afford the goal without straining current cash flows — and build bigger wealth over time.
